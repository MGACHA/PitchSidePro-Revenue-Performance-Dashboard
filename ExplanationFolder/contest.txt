The Dataviz World Champs start now. But this is more than just a challenge. This is your journey to Barcelona. :spain:

 

Finalists will earn a conference ticket and the opportunity to showcase their work live, on stage, in front of the global data community. The experience is designed around that moment. The lights, the audience, the energy of seeing your work come to life in person.

For Round 1, we’re diving into a football-inspired dataset (yes, in honor of the World Cup) and your challenge is simple to say… and hard to execute:

 

What is driving changes in revenue performance over time, and where should the business focus next to sustain growth?

 

You’re building for executives, you’ve got 5 pages, and every pixel needs to earn its place.


:soccer_ball: The scenario
 

For Round 1, you’ll be working with a fictional dataset from:

 

PitchSide Pro - a global e-commerce retailer specializing in football (soccer) merchandise.

 

Think:

Club kits and national team gear
Boots and training equipment
Scarves, collectibles, and all things fan culture
Over the past five years, the business has grown through a mix of:

seasonal demand
major football moments (:eyes: tournaments, finals, big wins)
product launches
digital channel expansion

There are peaks, patterns, and stories. Your job is to make sense of it all clearly.

:direct_hit: Your task

Build a Power BI report that answers:


What is driving changes in revenue performance over time, and where should the business focus next to sustain growth?


Simple question. Not a simple answer.


:busts_in_silhouette: Your audience

You are building for the executive leadership team. This is not a casual audience.


They:

check this report every day (yes, daily)
start with top-line performance
expect precision, not guesswork
want to drill into the numbers when something looks off

Your report should feel:

instantly clear at first glance
deep and trustworthy on interaction

:white_heavy_check_mark: Submission requirements

All submissions must:

Be built in Power BI Desktop
Be a single .pbix file
Contain no more than 5 pages
Use the provided dataset
Include at least three core Power BI visuals
Meet accessibility expectations
clear labels and alt text
strong contrast
intentional layout and reading order
How to submit:

.pbix file or Publish to Web URL

Add your submission questions to this thread and tag me!
 
:artist_palette: What you can do

This is where you can have some fun.


You’re encouraged to:

Use custom themes
Add images (flags, club badges, subtle backgrounds, etc.)
Bring in a bit of football energy and personality
If it helps tell the story better, go for it. Just remember: clarity comes first.


:prohibited: What you cannot do

To keep things fair and focused on insight:

Do not alter the dataset
no editing values
no adding rows
no modifying the underlying data
Do not bring in external data
Do not simulate or fabricate additional data
You can:

model the data
transform it
analyze it

But the source data itself must remain unchanged.


🧠 What we’re looking for

This is not about who can build the busiest report.


We’re looking for:

Clear explanation of what’s happening over time
Identification of real drivers of performance
Insight across:
products
regions
channels
customer segments
Strong, defensible recommendations
There isn’t one right answer. But there are clearer, stronger, more compelling ones.



🧩 The data

You’ll be working with:

FactSales - revenue, cost, profit
FactTraffic - sessions, conversions, behavior
Supported by:

Date
Product
Customer
Region
Channel
Everything you need is there.
