# Dashboard Screenshots Explained

This document explains what each dashboard screenshot shows and how to interpret the business story.

## 1) Executive Summary

### What this screenshot shows
- Net revenue is trending upward over the 5-year period.
- Conversion rate stays relatively stable around the low 3% range.
- Revenue spikes are linked to specific moments (for example holidays and football-related peaks).

### Key interpretation
- Sales growth appears to be driven mostly by higher volume, not a major jump in conversion efficiency.
- The business is growing, but conversion optimization is still a clear opportunity.

![Executive Summary](images/ExecutiveSummary.png)

## 2) Revenue Drivers Deep Dive

### What this screenshot shows
- Revenue by channel over time indicates both channels contribute strongly.
- Mobile App conversion performance is stronger than Web.
- Regional split shows EMEA as the largest revenue contributor, with Americas and APAC behind.
- Quarterly conversion is highest in Q4.

### Key interpretation
- Q4 seasonality and key football moments are major growth drivers.
- Web still has scale, but Mobile App has better efficiency, so channel strategy should balance both.

![Revenue Drivers](images/RevenueDrivers.png)

## 3) Regional Performance and Investment Opportunities

### What this screenshot shows
- EMEA leads total revenue share.
- Americas has meaningful revenue scale but lower conversion compared with other regions.
- APAC shows strong conversion efficiency and growth potential.
- Country-level bubble chart compares conversion rate against YoY growth.

### Key interpretation
- Regional sales are not identical: EMEA leads in size, APAC leads in efficiency, and Americas has the largest conversion upside.
- Best investment approach is region-specific, not one global strategy.

![Regional Performance](images/RegionalPerformance.png)

## 4) Channel Analysis: Efficiency, Growth, and Budget Focus

### What this screenshot shows
- Mobile App conversion rate is consistently above Web conversion rate.
- Both channels support total growth contribution.
- Trend lines suggest repeated seasonal/periodic peaks.

### Key interpretation
- Mobile should be treated as a high-efficiency channel.
- Web should be prioritized for funnel optimization because small conversion gains can unlock large revenue due to its scale.

![Channel Analysis](images/ChannelAnalysis.png)

## 5) Forward-Looking Insights

### What this screenshot shows
- Key moments align with conversion spikes.
- Revenue trend includes a forward forecast band, indicating continued positive trajectory with uncertainty range.
- Moment-type chart suggests transfers and holidays are strong conversion moments.

### Key interpretation
- Sales are expected to keep rising if the business executes around peak moments.
- The clearest next actions are campaign timing before peaks and conversion improvements in weaker funnel segments.

![Forward-Looking Insight](images/ForwardLookingInsight.png)

## Executive Takeaways

- Sales are increasing over time.
- Growth is currently more volume-led than conversion-led.
- Regional performance is mixed, with different priorities by market.
- Mobile App is more conversion-efficient; Web has larger optimization headroom.
- Peak football and seasonal moments should anchor campaign planning.
